THE HIMALAYAS

INDIA'S NORTHERN SPINE · CLIMATE SHIELD · GLACIAL RESERVE

—
LENGTH
West to east — Nanga Parbat to Namcha Barwa. Spans 5 nations.
—
GLACIERS
3rd largest ice reserve on Earth after Antarctica & the Arctic.

KANGCHENJUNGA
India's highest peak — world's 3rd highest mountain.
The Himalayas block icy Arctic winds from Central Asia and force monsoon clouds to rise, cool, and release rain — making Indian agriculture possible. Without them, India's climate would be unrecognisable.

Köppen Climate Classification

6 CLIMATE ZONES

EQUATORIAL RAINFOREST TO ARCTIC TUNDRA — WITHIN ONE NATION

Z01
🌿 TROPICAL WET

W. Ghats, Malabar, NE India. >18°C all year. Over 2,000mm rain.

Z02
🌦 TROPICAL SAVANNA

Most of peninsular India. Wet summers, dry winters.

Z03
🏜 SEMI-ARID

Rajasthan, Deccan rain shadow. Extreme temperature swings.

Z04
☀️ DESERT / ARID

Thar Desert. 0°C → 50°C+. Less than 250mm annual rainfall.

Z05
🌤 HUMID SUBTROPICAL

Indo-Gangetic Plain. Hot summers, cool winters. Most populous.

Z06
🏔 ALPINE / TUNDRA

High Himalayas & Ladakh. Below 0°C year-round. Snow.
